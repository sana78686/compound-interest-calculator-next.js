# ISA calculator — explained in simple English

This page is for people who **do not know UK investing abbreviations** yet. Sentences are short on purpose.

---

## First: what is an “ISA”?

**ISA** stands for **Individual Savings Account**. In the UK there are a few types. This site focuses on the idea of a **Stocks & Shares ISA** — a **wrapper** where **investments can grow** without UK **income tax and capital gains tax** on gains inside the ISA, subject to rules and limits set by the government.

You do **not** need to memorise tax law to use the calculator. Think of **ISA mode** as: *“I am saving inside the UK ISA subscription limits.”*

---

## What is the “£20,000” people talk about?

Each **UK tax year**, there is a **limit** on **new money** you can **subscribe** (pay in) into your ISAs **in total**, across the types you use.  

For this calculator we use the common **£20,000 per tax year** figure that applies to the adult ISA allowance in many cases (the real rules can change; always check official sources).

So: in the model, **extra monthly payments** cannot push **new subscriptions** above that yearly cap **in a single tax year**.

---

## What is a “tax year” here?

In the calculator, a UK **tax year** runs from **6 April** one year to **5 April** the next year.

When the timeline crosses **6 April**, the tool treats it as a **new tax year** and **resets** how much of the £20,000 “room” has been used for **new** subscriptions in that new year.

---

## What does “ISA mode” do in this app?

When you turn **ISA mode** on:

- The **compound interest maths** is the **same** as normal mode.
- **Monthly contributions** are treated as **subscriptions** into the ISA.
- If a monthly payment would go **over** what is still allowed in the **current** tax year, the extra part is **not** added to the balance (the tool tracks how much was “capped” so it can mention that in **Insights**).

So ISA mode is **not** a different interest formula — it adds a **simple UK subscription cap** on top of the same growth model.

---

## Currency and pounds

ISA limits in real life are set in **UK pounds (£)**. The calculator’s **£20,000 cap** is applied in **number terms** in the code. If you pick another **display currency**, the **same numeric cap** is still used in the model (this is a simplification for education, not a currency converter).
